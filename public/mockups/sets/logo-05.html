Precision minimal
Ultra-minimal engineering-instrument aesthetic. A single isometric solid — one printable part — drawn in fine, even, near-white strokes, with the three visible faces implied only by an interior Y of edges. To its right sits a fine vertical dimension line, complete with witness lines and serif tick caps: the universal annotation for a measured height. The whole composition is monochrome restraint, broken by exactly one small teal node at the dimension's midpoint — a measured point on a solid. Lots of negative space, one consistent stroke weight. At 16px it collapses to the bare solid plus a single dimension line, still unmistakably a part being measured.
