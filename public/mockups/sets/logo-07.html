Joinery
A wright is one who joins. The mark is a single solid block split along a dovetail seam into two parts that lock together — a teal piece keying into a warm amber piece, fitted so precisely the seam reads as one object. The teal part's tall left edge and the interlock subtly trace a P. Shaded top faces over darker sides give it real dimension — the satisfying click of two parts that finally fit.
