Negative-P · chamfered
The P pocket is cut into a thick aluminum billet that's been finish-milled with a chamfer tool around its entire perimeter — the kind of part a machinist makes when they want a production piece to look like it cost more to make than it did. A wide angled bevel ring separates the full-size sides from the inset top face, catching the overhead key light at a shallow angle and glowing distinctly brighter than either the main face or the sides. The industrial rx=4 corners and condensed P letterform say billet, not consumer electronics.
