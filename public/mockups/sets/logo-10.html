Negative-P · machined
The team-favorite negative-space P, but rendered as a real machined part: a deep pocket bored clean into a glossy solid teal block. Dramatic top-down key light pours a teal→deep-navy gradient across the face; the pocket walls carry a soft occlusion shadow so the cut reads as genuine 3D depth, not a flat knockout. A crisp specular catches one top edge, a single warm amber spark glints off the upper corner, and a faint contact shadow grounds the block. The P-counter survives as a small raised teal boss inside the pocket. At 16 px the lighting flattens to a clean, legible knockout. Just off the CNC.
