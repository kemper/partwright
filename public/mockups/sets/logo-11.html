Negative-P · double-take
The team-favorite negative-space P, given a sly optical double-meaning. The same die-cut letter is bored into a teal tile — but the cut has real isometric depth: the P-shaped pocket sinks into the block, side-walls raked down-and-right in a darker teal under a crisp machined lip. The eye flips between a flat letter and a 3D part. And the bowl's counter isn't just a hole — it's a small teal boss standing proud inside the pocket, lit cap, drop-shadowed wall, one warm amber spark: a part seated in its socket. Unmistakably a P; at 16 px the depth flattens to a clean knockout.
