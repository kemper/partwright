## Key decisions

**Diagnosis.** The existing catalog model was a *two interleaved ribbons* split
(`componentCount === 2`, a cone cut by one full-diameter helical slab). A retro
from the original author confirmed they had *retreated* from a nested-screw
design to that simpler one under iteration pressure. The user is right that it's
the wrong archetype: the reference is a multi-start **screw & nut** with a
distinct inner core and outer shell.

**Construction.** Inner SCREW = a rounded 5-lobe star cross-section lofted with
twist + taper (the 5 lobes become 5 helical threads; its base is the 5-point
star from the reference). Outer NUT = full cone minus the screw's clearance
envelope, then opened into a spiral cage by subtracting a proportionally-larger
star cutter (breaks the wall over the lobes, leaves valley ribs) between a solid
base collar and a solid top cap so all ribs stay one connected piece.

**The hard part — keeping the two parts FREE.** Building the clearance gap by
`offset(clearance)` then a tapered `extrude` scales the gap down to ~0.15 mm at
the narrow tip — far below the mesh edge length — so the screw and nut welded
into one component every time (verified: clearance sweeps and 396k-triangle mesh
refinement both failed to separate them). The fix was a `Curves.loft` of
**constant-offset** sections, so the ~0.5 mm gap holds at every height. This is
the same principle the old 2-ribbon model relied on (a constant-thickness
cutter) generalized to a star tube. Verified `componentCount === 2` with no
fusion warnings, and a cutaway render confirmed a continuous clearance moat.

**Robustness.** Capped the sliders (starts ≤ 6, turns ≤ 2.0, clearance ≥ 0.4)
after an 8-corner sweep — beyond those bounds crowded lobes re-fuse. Default
(20 mm base, 55 mm, 5 starts, 1.35 turns, 0.5 mm) is clean at 35k triangles,
well under the catalog budget.

**Workflow.** Used the new `npm run model:preview` CLI (~2 s headless
Node+WASM) for ~15 fast iterations — exactly the "isolate-the-variable"
discipline the prior retro begged for; it turned a topology bug that took the
original author 7 browser round-trips into a handful of CLI probes. The catalog
`.partwright.json` (real thumbnail + geometryData) was regenerated by driving
the app's own `exportSession` through a throwaway Playwright spec, then verified
by clicking the catalog tile and confirming it imports + renders as 2 parts.

**Generalization (deferred).** The model is structured around a swappable 2D
profile (`star`) fed to generic `helixStar`/cage machinery, so the same approach
generalizes to any profile (rectangle, cylinder, arbitrary silhouette) — a
"twistify into a print-in-place bolt & nut" operation. Left as a proposed
follow-up rather than scoped into this catalog PR.
