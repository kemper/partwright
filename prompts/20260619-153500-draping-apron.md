## Key decisions

**Why the conforming shell looked like underwear**: the first fix made the panel
a body-offset *shell* clipped to the front — it hugged the groin/hips. That
removed the pass-through but read as briefs.

**Draping is 100% SDF** (answered the design Q): a drape is a flat thin SHEET
that sits just in front of the body's *measured* forward-most point and hangs
straight DOWN. The measurement is the key — march the body's own SDF
(`body.evaluate(0,y,z)` until sign flip) to find the belly apex, place the sheet
just proud of it, then hang vertical. Below the belly the legs recede, so the
sheet is automatically left hanging away from them = "body separation". No cloth
sim needed; folds would be an optional noise displacement.

**buildPanel rewrite — two fits**: `fit:'drape'` (new default, the hanging sheet)
and `fit:'hug'` (the old conforming shell, kept for tabards pressed flat). Added
`over` (a node/array of under-garments folded into the apex measurement so the
sheet clears them — without it a thick jacket can bury the sheet) and exposed
`thickness` as the printable sheet wall.

**buildApron**: a real chef bib apron — narrow bib flaring into a wider skirt to
the shin (two drape sheets, same params so their faces align and weld), held by a
NECK halter + WAIST ties (`ties:true` default). The straps are what let a real
apron stand off the body; they also guarantee one printable component.

**Added `evaluate` to the figure `Node` interface** (the concrete sdf nodes
already have it) so the apex measurement type-checks.

Updated the chef example + catalog code to `F.clothing.apron(rig, { over:
[jacket, pants], bottom: 'shin', thickness: r.chestY*0.2 })`, rewrote the panel
unit tests for drape semantics (proud+welded, front/back/both, hug still
conforms, halter + ties present/absent, validation), and refreshed figure.md +
the ClothingApi JSDoc. Verified: manifold, 1 component, apron paints cleanly, no
warnings; typecheck + 1488 unit tests + acyclic deps all green.
