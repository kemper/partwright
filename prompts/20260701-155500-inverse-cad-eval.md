## Key decisions

**Built the metric side of the loop.** `invariants.mjs` (bbox + PCA + RANSAC
sphere-fit), `render.mjs` (shared-bbox target-vs-candidate composer, so both
grids share framing and are directly comparable), `eval.mjs` (CLI: target
STL + candidate JS → chamfer/hausdorff + comparison PNG), `montage.mjs`
(contact-sheet composer), and `renderAllTargets.mjs` (one-shot to
visualize what each STL actually contains).

**Ran hips as the first spike and it converged in 2 iterations.** v1 (flat
bar + 3 socket cavities, my prior architectural guess) → chamfer 0.49 mm,
hausdorff 2.57 mm. v2 (3 balls + struts, corrected from the target render)
→ chamfer 0.16 mm, hausdorff 0.56 mm. Comparison PNG shows near-identical
silhouettes.

**Discovered my previous Dummy 13 architecture was INVERTED.** The eval
loop's target-only render of `frame_hips.stl` shows 3 BALLS with struts,
not sockets in a flat bar. My last session's least-squares sphere fits were
picking up ball *outsides*, and I misread them as *cavity* insides. Also
saw in the other target renders that:
- Bodies (hips/waist/abdomen/neck) mostly have BALLS
- Bridge pieces (hip/shoulder, knee/elbow) are what hold the SOCKETS
- Chest and head have a MIX (sockets on some faces, balls on others)
- Upper arm, hands are structurally complex with print-in-place slots
This is the opposite of what `src/geometry/dummy13.ts` on PR #876 assumes,
and it explains "the complete disaster."

**Rendering bug caught: `sharp(...).png()` inside a Promise-parallel
compose was returning empty buffers.** The first comparison PNG came out
blank. Fixed by calling `.png().toBuffer()` inline before Promise.all,
and folding all overlays (target row, candidate row, label SVG) into a
single `.composite()` call.

**Composer intentionally shares bbox across both rows.** Otherwise each
sharpener zooms to its own extent and a candidate with 90 % of the target's
size looks the same as the target — hiding proportional errors. `unionBBox`
of both meshes' bboxes is used for both `composePng` calls.
