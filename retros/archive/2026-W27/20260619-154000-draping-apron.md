# Retro — draping front/back-panel garments (PR #752)

Task: fix the chef's apron passing through the body, then (on iteration) make it a
real *draping* apron and generalize a panel garment family.

## Liked
- `model:preview` with `--view`/`--views` + reading the PNG inline made the
  iterate-on-shape loop fast: I could see the speedo-vs-drape problem immediately
  and confirm each construction (conform → shell → flat sheet) in ~one render each.
- The `figure:smoke`/0-triangle-label warning caught a buried apron (paints
  nothing) *before* a bake — exactly when I'd offset the panel under the jacket.

## Lacked
- No way to drive the browser visually in this env (no Playwright MCP); fine for
  headless `model:preview`, but colour/paint correctness still needs the slow xvfb
  bake, so I verified shape headlessly and trusted labels for colour.
- `mix()` and other figure-internal helpers aren't exposed to model code, so
  scratch snippets that mirror builder math have to re-inline them (minor friction,
  hit it twice).

## Learned
- "Conforming = always fits" solved pass-through but produced the *underwear* look;
  the user wanted **drape** = a flat sheet hung from the body's measured forward
  apex, going vertical below the belly so the legs recede away from it. Body
  separation falls out for free once the sheet stops conforming and hangs.
- A drape that floats off the body must be **connected** deliberately — either the
  sheet's back welds into the apex (thickness > standoff) or via straps (neck
  halter + waist ties). Straps are the realistic AND the topological fix.
- A thin SDF sheet aliases into a marching-cubes grid when its thickness ≈
  edgeLength — thicken the sheet (or refine) rather than chase z-fighting.

## Longed for
- A subjective-shape task like "make it look like a chef apron" is exactly the
  "prototype 2–3 and let the user pick" case in CLAUDE.md — I did sequential
  iterations instead. A quick `compare`-style A/B of (conform | flat | bib+skirt)
  up front would've converged in one round instead of three.
- A headless way to verify *paint/colour* (not just labels) without the ~75s xvfb
  bake would close the last verification gap for figure work.
