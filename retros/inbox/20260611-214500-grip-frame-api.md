## Liked / Worked
- **The user's question WAS the design.** They asked "can the API know the XYZ
  of the palm to connect things to?" — naming the exact missing primitive. The
  internals already computed the palm normal (`cross(hinge, foreDir)`) and threw
  it away; exposing it as a `GripFrame { point, palmNormal, gripAxis, reach }`
  was a small, additive change that dissolved a bug I'd fought for THREE prior
  rounds by eyeballing FK. Lesson: when a figurine pose keeps missing, the fix is
  usually a missing *anatomical anchor*, not more angle-guessing.
- **Deriving `eulerAlignZ` from the engine's actual rotation matrix** (read
  `opRotate`: R = Rz·Ry·Rx, so with rz=0 `R·[0,0,1] = [cx·sy, −sx, cx·cy]`)
  gave a closed-form axis→Euler instead of trial-and-error sign-flipping. Worth
  reading the matrix rather than guessing conventions.
- **Delegating the rocker's first pass to `model-sculpt`** kept ~10 preview PNGs
  out of my context; I only Read the 2–3 final candidates to make the call.

## Lacked
- **A bounds gotcha cost a test-fix round:** a capsule's AABB extent is
  `length + 2·radius`, so my `toBeCloseTo(6)` should have been `7`. Obvious in
  hindsight; a one-line note "capsule/sphere bounds include the radius" near the
  primitive factories would have saved it.
- **Two-handed props don't fit `holdAt`.** A guitar spans two grips, but `holdAt`
  aligns to ONE. I had to document "build from both grip points" as prose. A
  `F.spanGrips(gL, gR, …)` or a two-anchor place helper would be the real
  primitive for instruments/bows/rifles — filed mentally as a follow-up.

## Learned
- **Adding a waist section, not a bigger blend, is what makes a guitar body read
  as a guitar.** A large `smoothUnion` between two bouts melts them into one oval
  (a paddle); a third pinched cylinder with SMALL blends preserves the figure-8.
  Same trick applies to any hourglass/figure-8 silhouette.
- **A shallower forward-facing plate is genus-2; a deeper one tunnels through the
  curved torso skin to genus-4.** Depth vs. topology is the real constraint when
  embedding a prop into an organic surface — not the silhouette.

## Longed for
- **`F.spanGrips` / a two-grip prop helper** (see Lacked) — the natural next API
  after the single-hand grip frame, for any two-handed instrument or weapon.
- **A `figure.poseProbe(rig)` / `model:preview --joints`** dumping world joint
  AND grip-frame positions as JSON — I still hand-rolled a `throw
  JSON.stringify(...)` scratch to read `grip.L.point`. (Repeated from the prior
  retro; the grip frame makes a first-class readout even more useful.)
