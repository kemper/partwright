# Rubric — cuirass / breastplate (Worn conformal-shell mode)

Score the candidate's armor against the reference. Each bullet is one judged item;
the judge must say whether it matches and, if not, the specific geometry fix.

- Reads as a rigid PLATE breastplate covering the chest and abdomen — not a soft padded vest or just an inflated/fat torso
- Sits clearly OVER the shirt (a distinct outer shell), with the shirt sleeves/collar still visible beneath it
- A defined top edge (collar/neckline) and a defined bottom edge (fauld/waist line) — crisp rims, not melted blobs
- A raised central keel ridge or chest definition gives the breastplate a forged, 3-D front (not a smooth featureless dome)
- Distinct pauldron caps sit on the shoulders, reading as separate plate pieces over the shoulder joint
- The armor wraps the torso in 3-D (front and back), symmetric left-to-right
- Chunky/printable; the figure does not look obese because of the armor
