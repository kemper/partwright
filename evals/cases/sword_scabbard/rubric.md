# Rubric — sword (Held) + scabbard (Hung), with belt (Ringed)

Score the candidate against the reference. Each bullet is one judged item; the
judge must say whether it matches and, if not, the specific geometry fix.

- A sword is held in one fist: a clear blade, a cross-guard perpendicular to the blade, a grip, and a pommel — reading as a sword, not a plain rod
- The sword seats IN the closed hand (grip across the fingers), not impaling the palm or floating beside it, and fuses to the fist as one piece
- The blade does not clip through the body, head, or legs
- An (empty) scabbard hangs at the hip, angled naturally downward, with a throat collar at the top and a closed tip
- The scabbard reads as a long sheath proportional to the blade (not a stubby pouch) and connects to the belt/hip
- A belt encircles the waist (the anchor the scabbard hangs from)
- All parts are chunky/printable and the whole reads as one fused warrior figure
