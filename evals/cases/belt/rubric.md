# Rubric — belt + buckle (Ringed attachment mode)

Score the candidate's belt against the reference. Each bullet is one judged item;
the judge must say whether it matches and, if not, the specific geometry fix.

- A continuous closed band encircles the waist (visible across the front and around the sides), following the body's elliptical cross-section
- The band sits OVER the pants/clothing (proud of the surface), not sunk into or floating away from the body
- A buckle reads at the front centre — a distinct plate clearly proud of the band, wider/taller than the band tube
- The band is an even thickness all the way around (no lumpy/fragmented segments), chunky enough to print
- The belt sits at a believable waist height (at the waistline, not up on the ribs or down on the hips)
- Left-right symmetric around the body
