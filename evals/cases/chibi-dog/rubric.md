# Rubric — chibi dog figurine (cute puppy style)

Score the candidate render against a cute chibi puppy target (a sitting puppy
with an oversized head, big expressive eyes, a soft snout, and clear dog ears).
No pinned reference image yet — judge against this rubric (a consistent pinned
reference set can be added later). Each bullet is one judged item; the judge must
say whether it matches and, if not, the specific geometry change that would fix
it. Renders shade by surface normal — judge FORM/PROPORTION (iris/pupil are real
geometry caps; color is applied at bake time, so do not penalize grey shading).

- Head is oversized — roughly half the silhouette mass — sitting over a small, rounded body (chibi, not realistic proportions)
- Eyes are large, round, and forward-facing — a dominant cute feature with a clear iris cap + distinct pupil cap (not small/beady button eyes or blank domes)
- Eyes sit near the vertical mid-face with the muzzle below, spaced with a soft gap
- A distinct projecting snout/muzzle with a small nose at its tip — clearly a dog snout, not a flat cat face or a flush cheek bulge
- Dog ears that read unmistakably: either long floppy drooping ears beside the head OR upright triangular perky ears — thick enough at the base to print (no needle tips, no knife-thin slabs)
- Slim tail with printable thickness, positioned so it reads from a rear-3/4 view (not a thin spike, not a hidden hip-blob)
- Front paws read as a pair sitting together at the front of the base (sitting-dog pose)
- Overall soft, rounded, continuous "plush" silhouette — no primitive-soup balls/tubes, no hard weld seams or pinches
- Sits flat and stable on a level base (no rocking bowl underside); nothing pokes below the base plane
