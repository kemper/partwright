# Rubric — chibi cat figurine (cute "Pixar-kitten" style)

Score the candidate render against the reference set (`reference.png`: a sitting
3/4, a face close-up, and a 3/4-with-yarn — all the same big-eyed cute-kitten
style). Each bullet is one judged item; the judge must say whether it matches
and, if not, the specific geometry change that would fix it. Renders shade by
surface normal, so judge FORM/PROPORTION (iris/pupil are real geometry caps;
color/stripes are applied at bake time — do not penalize the grey shading).

- Head is oversized — roughly half the silhouette mass — sitting over a small, rounded body (chibi, not realistic proportions)
- Eyes are VERY large, round, and forward-facing — together spanning a big fraction of the face width — the dominant cute feature (not small/beady button eyes)
- Each eye shows a clear iris cap and a distinct pupil cap (and reads as glossy/expressive), not a blank dome
- Eyes sit near the vertical mid-face with the muzzle below them, spaced with a soft gap (not high-set or pinched together)
- Small, soft, pale muzzle pad with a tiny triangular nose on it — a dainty cat muzzle, not a large pig/teddy snout
- Upright triangular ears with a visible scooped inner-ear region (the pink-inner-ear cue), thick enough at the base to print (no needle tips)
- Slim tail that curls around to the side/front of the body, with a printable thickness (not a thin spike, not a hidden hip-blob)
- Front paws read as a pair sitting together at the front of the base (sitting-cat pose), not a single side-cantilever limb
- Overall soft, rounded, continuous "plush" silhouette — no primitive-soup balls/tubes, no hard weld seams or pinches
- Sits flat and stable on a level base (no rocking bowl underside); nothing pokes below the base plane
