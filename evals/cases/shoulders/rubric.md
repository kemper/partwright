# Rubric — shoulders / deltoid

Score the candidate's shoulder region against the reference. Each bullet is one
judged item; the judge must say whether it matches and, if not, the specific
geometry change that would fix it.

- Deltoid forms a distinct rounded cap over the shoulder joint where the arm meets the torso (not a straight cylinder-into-box seam)
- Shoulder width reads as roughly 2.2–2.6 head-widths for this stocky adult build (not narrow/sloped)
- The trapezius slope from neck to shoulder is gradual, not a flat horizontal shelf
- The arm-to-torso transition is a smooth weld with no pinch, gap, or hard crease at the armpit
- Clavicle line is suggested across the upper chest (subtle ridge, not absent and not a hard groove)
- Shoulders are symmetric left-to-right at matched mass and height
