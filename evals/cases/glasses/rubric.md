# Rubric — eyeglasses (Perched attachment mode)

Score the candidate's glasses against the reference. Each bullet is one judged
item; the judge must say whether it matches and, if not, the specific geometry
change that would fix it.

- Two round lens rims, one centred in front of each eye, roughly equal in size and symmetric left-to-right
- The lenses stand proud of the face (a visible gap to the eye surface), not flush/embedded in the skin
- A bridge connects the two rims over the nose at the right height (across the upper nose, not down on the tip or up on the brow)
- A temple arm runs from the outer edge of each rim back to the ear, continuous and unbroken (no floating gaps or fragmented segments)
- The temple arms hug the side of the head rather than cutting a straight diagonal through open air
- The rims and arms are chunky enough to read and print (no wispy/vanishing thin tubes), but not so thick they swallow the eyes
- The eyes (iris/pupil) remain visible through/within the lens rims
